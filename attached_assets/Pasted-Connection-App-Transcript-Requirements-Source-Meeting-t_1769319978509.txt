Connection App – Transcript & Requirements

Source: Meeting transcript (Jan 24, 2026) with Alex Azzi + Raed Marji.

This document contains:
	1.	Key transcript extracts (what was said)
	2.	Structured requirements (what to build / improve)

⸻

1) Key Transcript Extracts (Highlights)

Flow / Copy
	•	“Not now” breaks the flow → rename CTA (e.g., “Start a 60-second experiment”, “Let’s connect deeper”).
	•	Remove “2026” from the UI copy.

Privacy
	•	Replace generic “Private by default…” with a dedicated Privacy section.
	•	Add a clear link to the privacy policy (Burner-values inspired).

Tone Grid
	•	Make Balance the visual default when centered.
	•	Increase Balance label prominence.
	•	Add clearer instruction: “Set the conversation tone by tapping anywhere on the grid.”

Eye Contact / Presence
	•	Add a reminder right after starting: put the phone down and look into each other’s eyes.

Question Mechanics
	•	Original idea: both people answer the same question.
	•	Concern: repeating answers feels awkward / unnatural.
	•	Alternative: pre-configured answers may be shared later (e.g., via email) without biasing the other person.

Capture & Memory
	•	Email capture should be prominent and early (“don’t make me think”).
	•	Desire: profile/history view of answers.
	•	Capture partner name (optional) to keep context.

Personalization & Ownership
	•	Users should be able to create their own flows:
	•	choose categorized sample questions
	•	type their own questions
	•	describe intention → generate suggested questions → pick 3 of 5
	•	customize colors
	•	share a link

Voice-First + Music
	•	Move toward a voice-first experience: app speaks questions + transcribes answers.
	•	Add simple toggles: Voice On/Off, Music On/Off.

Mini CRM / Connections
	•	After delivering value, capture contact details:
	•	photo (business card / phone number OCR)
	•	or speak contact details
	•	Store answers + contact + event context; enable query/search later.

⸻

2) Structured Requirements (Prioritized)

P0 – Must Ship Next

R1. Fix Broken Flow + Copy
	•	Replace “Not now” with a more natural CTA.
	•	Remove “2026” from UI text.
	•	Fix any glitch impacting progression.

Acceptance: No dated copy remains; flow completes without confusing states.

R2. Privacy Section + Link
	•	Dedicated privacy section + link to policy.
	•	Clear promise: “Nothing shared without permission.”

Acceptance: Privacy link visible before starting; language is explicit.

R3. Tone Grid: Balance as Default
	•	When centered, highlight “Balance” and make it larger.
	•	Improve instruction text for first-time users.

Acceptance: New users understand the grid without explanation.

R4. In-Flow Eye Contact Reminder
	•	One short interstitial: “Put the phone down and look into each other’s eyes.”

Acceptance: Shows once per session; one-tap continue.

R5. Email Capture Placement
	•	Prominent “Email this to yourself” early.

Acceptance: Appears within first 1–2 screens; no scrolling required.

⸻

P1 – Strong Next Iteration

R6. Question Mechanics Modes
	•	Default mode that avoids “interrogation vs confession”.
	•	Optional toggle for “both answer” (if kept).
	•	If both-answer: add voice isolation/diarization.

R7. Profile / History
	•	Sessions list: timestamp, tone, answers, optional partner name.

R8. Flow Builder
	•	Create flows via custom questions, categorized templates, or intent → AI suggestions.
	•	Pick 3 of 5 suggested questions.
	•	Basic theme customization.
	•	Shareable link.

⸻

P2 – Differentiators

R9. Voice-First Mode
	•	TTS asks questions; STT captures answers.

R10. Ambient Music Toggle
	•	Music on/off; ensure it doesn’t interfere with speech capture.

R11. Connections CRM
	•	Capture contact details after the interaction.
	•	Store by event; searchable later.

⸻

3) Minimal Data Model (Guidance)
	•	User
	•	Flow (questions + theme)
	•	Session (flow_id, timestamp, tone, answers, optional partner_name, optional event)
	•	Connection (optional contact info linked to session/event)
